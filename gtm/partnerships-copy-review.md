# Partnerships Page — Copy Review

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Page:** /partnerships
**Audience:** Supply-side. Business owners (cafes, packaging centers, factories, retail, farms, mechanic shops), community operators, individual creators with archives — people whose workforces already do the visual work that frontier AI labs need.
**Lenses applied:** Transformational Landing Pages, Direct Response Copy, Positioning, Marketing Psychology.

---

## Diagnosis (one paragraph)

The page is written for the people who already work in AI data — "modalities," "VLA pretraining," "domain randomization," "co-supply." The brief says we're targeting people who run cafes and packaging centers. Those people don't read "non-exclusive training rights to underlying footage" and think "that's me." The hero ("Sell us your data") implies the visitor already has data. Cafe owners don't think they have data — they think they have employees. Until we name the visitor by what they actually are (a business owner with a workforce that does visible work) and reframe what they have (footage of work = training data), the rest of the page is just a vendor agreement they don't know they need.

---

## 1. Top 5 Highest-Leverage Copy Improvements

### 1. Hero headline — "Sell us your data" excludes 90% of the target

**Wrong now:** "Sell us your data." A cafe owner does not believe she has "data." She has baristas, a back-of-house, and security cameras. The current headline is product-aware copy (Schwartz Level 4) shipped to a problem-unaware audience (Level 2). It also fronts a verb the visitor doesn't yet trust ("Sell" before any value has been built).

**Change to:**
> **Headline:** Your team already makes the data AI labs are paying for.
> **Subhead:** If your business runs cameras — kitchens, lines, floors, fields, bays, registers — frontier AI labs will pay you for the footage. We license what you have, pay you to capture more, or co-supply with our workforce.

**Why it converts:** Pattern interrupt + recognition. "Your team already makes" reframes a non-event (Tuesday at the cafe) into an asset they already own. "Already" is doing heavy lifting — it removes the activation cost. It also passes the 5-second "this is for me" test for the entire long-tail of physical-workforce businesses. The subhead does the Schwartz lift from problem-aware to solution-aware in one sentence (kitchens/lines/floors/fields/bays/registers), then names the three deals so scanners get it without scrolling.

---

### 2. Stats are about Claru, not the partner — flip them to outcomes

**Wrong now:** `3M+ annotations · 386K egocentric clips · 100+ cities, 5+ countries`. These prove Claru is real, but a cafe owner reads "annotations" and bounces. They prove our throughput, not their payoff.

**Change to (under hero, replacing current strip):**
> **5+ frontier labs** *buying right now* · **Net-15** *paid on signed license* · **Weekly payouts** *for active capture*

**Why it converts:** Specificity + self-interest (Caples rule 1). "5+ frontier labs buying right now" creates the starving-crowd signal (Halbert) — demand is real, urgent, and named. Net-15 and weekly payouts are concrete promises that anchor the deal in money the visitor understands. Claru's volume stats belong on a "Why us" credibility strip lower on the page, not as the hero proof.

---

### 3. Section 1 ("What we partner on") leads with modality jargon, not the visitor

**Wrong now:** "Egocentric Video / Synthetic / Robotics / Vision + Multimodal." A franchise owner has no idea which modality her drive-thru window is.

**Change the section header to:**
> **Heading:** If your business looks like this, labs want it.
> **Sub:** We pay for footage of real work in real environments. Below is what's selling now.

Then change the four card titles from modality names to **business archetypes** the visitor recognizes:

- **01 — Hands-on work in real environments** (was "Egocentric Video"): Kitchens, workshops, salons, mechanic bays, packaging lines, farms, retail floors. POV, fixed-camera, or smart-glasses. Hands in frame. Long takes.
- **02 — Cameras already running** (was Vision/Multimodal): If you already record for QA, training, or security, we license the archive. Months of footage = days of revenue.
- **03 — Capture teams + creators** (was Synthetic): Active shooters with rigs, drones, smart-glasses, action-sports archives. We pay per hour of usable raw.
- **04 — Robotics + manipulation** (kept): Teleop, exoskeleton, multi-camera + depth + IMU. For partners with engineering teams.

**Why it converts:** This is Jobs-to-be-Done positioning. The visitor self-identifies in 5 seconds because they recognize their *situation*, not the buyer's taxonomy. The modality names can stay as small subtitles inside each card for SEO, but the leading title must be human.

---

### 4. DealTypes is structured for AI buyers, not first-time partners

**Wrong now:** "Archive License / Paid Commission / Co-Supply" is correct but cold. The "best for" footers (BPOs, "robotics startups, dataset vendors with workforce gaps") tell most visitors they're at the wrong door.

**Change the headline + headlines per card:**
> **Section heading:** Three ways we pay you. Pick the one that fits.

- **01 — License what you already have.** *You have footage. We pay for the right to train on it.* Non-exclusive. You keep your reels, channels, and security recordings. Pays on signature, **net-15**.
- **02 — Get paid to capture.** *We send the spec. You shoot. We pay weekly.* Per-hour rates by modality. No editing required. We handle review and rejection.
- **03 — Bring your workforce. We bring the buyers.** *You have people doing real work. We have AI labs buying it.* We supply the spec, the QC, and the payout rail. You supply the workforce. Revenue share or per-hour.

Drop the current "// best for" footer with the BPO language. Replace with one line:

> **Typical first deal:** $5K–$50K archive license, or $40–$120/hr capture rate by modality.

**Why it converts:** Each headline is now the answer to "what do I do, what do I get?" — written from the partner's POV. The pricing line removes the biggest friction in B2B buying ("am I even in the right ballpark?") — Hopkins' specificity principle and an anchor (marketing-psychology: anchoring effect). Round-number ranges signal "real deal" without claiming a number we can't honor.

---

### 5. FitCriteria is gatekeeping the wrong people

**Wrong now:** Section 03 lists three criteria — "video we don't have," "distribution to AI labs," "annotation capacity." Two of three only apply to dataset vendors and BPOs. A bakery chain reads "warm intros to Anthropic" and leaves.

**Change to:**
> **Heading:** Who we say yes to.
> **Sub:** Be honest about which one of these you are. It gets you a faster reply.

- **01 — You operate a workforce that does visible work.** Cafes, packaging, manufacturing, farms, retail, trades, fleets. Your cameras already see something a model wants to learn.
- **02 — You own footage we can't get from public datasets.** Specific environments, regions, demographics, or tasks. Niche beats volume.
- **03 — You can ship capture on a schedule.** Active creators, capture teams, regional collection partners. You hit the spec, you get paid weekly.
- **04 — You sell to AI labs we don't reach yet.** Distribution partners, BPOs adding AI data, dataset vendors with workforce gaps.

**Why it converts:** Disqualification works (direct-response: "velvet rope effect") — but only if the *first criterion* is the largest, most-recognizable group. By leading with operators (#1) we tell the cafe owner "yes, you" before we filter out the wrong people. We also inverted the implicit message from "you must already be in this industry" to "if you have a workforce, you're in."

## 2. Hero Rewrite — 3 Alternatives

Each pairing is written to compete with the current "Sell us your data" / "We license archives, commission new capture, and co-supply with our workforce."

### Alternative A — *Recognition / problem-aware*
> **Headline:** Your team already makes the data AI labs are paying for.
> **Subhead:** Frontier labs pay for footage of real work in real environments — kitchens, lines, floors, fields, bays, registers. We license what you've already recorded, pay you to capture more, or run capture with our workforce inside yours.
> **Primary CTA:** See if your business qualifies →
> **Secondary:** partners@claru.ai

### Alternative B — *Money-forward / outcome-first*
> **Headline:** Get paid for the footage your business already records.
> **Subhead:** Five frontier AI labs are buying training data right now. If you run a workforce — packaging, manufacturing, food service, trades, retail, agriculture — what your cameras see is what they're paying for.
> **Primary CTA:** Apply to partner →
> **Secondary:** See deal terms

### Alternative C — *Direct challenge / pattern interrupt*
> **Headline:** You've got training data. You just don't know it yet.
> **Subhead:** If your business uses cameras — security, QA, POV, drone, dash, body — that footage is what frontier AI labs train on. We license, commission, or co-supply. Net-15 on signature.
> **Primary CTA:** Apply to partner →
> **Secondary:** What kind of footage qualifies?

**Recommendation:** Ship **Alternative A**. It's the highest-clarity hero for the broadest target without being clickbait. B is sharper for paid-traffic landing variants. C tests well as a Twitter/X-driven referral page where the audience is already intrigued.

## 4. Positioning Sharpening

### Is the page clear on WHO it's for vs. WHO it's not for?
**No.** The brief says cafes, packaging centers, factories, farms, mechanic shops. The current page reads like it was written for dataset vendors and creators. The 5-second test fails for the primary audience — a cafe owner cannot get from "Sell us your data" + "Egocentric Video / Synthetic / Robotics / Vision+Multimodal" to "this is for me." She bounces before scroll.

The fix is structural, not cosmetic: **lead the page with the operator archetype, demote the AI-industry partner archetype to a secondary path.**

### Is the value prop unambiguous?
**No.** The page explains *what we do* (license, commission, co-supply), not *what they get* (money for things their team already does). Direct-response rule: lead with the reader's transformation, not the vendor's process. "We license, commission, and co-supply" is a feature list dressed as a value prop.

The unambiguous value prop is one line:
> **You already make this. We pay for it.**

That's the sentence the entire page should be in service of.

### Does it earn "this is for me" in <5 seconds?
**Currently no, for the primary target.** With the Alternative A hero + business-archetype card titles + operator-first fit criteria + price anchors, yes — the page passes the test for cafes, packagers, factories, farms, retailers, and fleets, while still preserving paths for creators, BPOs, and dataset vendors as secondary audiences.

### Audience hierarchy the page should encode (in this order)
1. **Operators with workforces** (largest TAM, lowest awareness — needs the most reframing). Lead the page.
2. **Creators and capture teams** (mid TAM, mid awareness — already think of themselves as having footage). Card 03.
3. **Dataset vendors / BPOs / robotics labs** (smallest TAM, highest awareness — already in the industry, ready to deal). Card 04 + a section near the FAQ.

Right now the page is ordered roughly 3 → 2 → 1, which is exactly backward.

## Post-Review Edit: Camera-Disqualification Fixes (applied 2026-05-05)

The original report assumed visitors already have cameras. They often don't — Claru ships rigs as part of capture deals. Edits applied to avoid disqualifying camera-less operators:

### Hero subhead (Alternative A — final shipped version)
> Frontier labs pay for footage of real work in real environments — kitchens, lines, floors, fields, bays, registers. We license what you've already recorded, **send cameras and** pay you to capture more, or run capture with our workforce inside yours.

(Inserted "send cameras and" — closes the loophole in one phrase.)

### ModalityGrid Card 02 (was "Cameras already running")
**Renamed to:** *"Footage you already have — or footage we'll capture for you"*
**Body:** "If you already record for QA, training, or security, we license the archive. If you don't, we ship the rigs and pay you per hour. Months of footage = days of revenue."
**Bullets** include explicit *"No cameras yet? We send them."*

### DealTypes Card 03 (Co-Supply)
Renamed from *"Bring your workforce. We bring the buyers"* to **"Bring your workforce. We bring everything else."** Body now leads with the cameras-included offer: *"We supply the cameras, the spec, the QC, and the payout rail."*

### DealTypes Card 02 (Get paid to capture)
Body now ends with *"We can ship cameras if you don't have them."*

### FitCriteria criterion 01 (operator path)
Body changed from *"Your cameras already see something a model wants to learn"* to *"We license what your cameras have already captured — or send cameras and capture with you."*

### New FAQ entry
> *"What if my business doesn't already have cameras?"*
> "Common — and not a blocker. We ship the rigs (chest-cam, smart-glasses, fixed mounts depending on the spec), set them up, and pay you per hour or revenue-share. Capture starts within ~2 weeks of a signed agreement."

**Net effect:** every camera mention now either describes a partner archetype that *might* have them, or explicitly names "we ship them" as the alternative. No path disqualifies a workforce-having operator on cameras alone.

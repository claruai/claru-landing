## Overview
Rainbow Robotics is a South Korean robotics company founded by KAIST researchers who built Korea's first bipedal robot. Their RB-Y1 humanoid platform is used by MIT, UC Berkeley, University of Washington, and Georgia Tech for research. Samsung became their largest shareholder (35%) in Jan 2025 with a $181M investment, accelerating humanoid development. Part of Korea's K-Humanoid Alliance (government-backed $770M initiative through 2030).

## Data Gap / Relationship
RB-Y1 is deployed heavily in research institutions — which means Rainbow Robotics and their university partners are generating training data. But with Samsung's backing and a push toward commercial deployment, they'll need large-scale annotated human demonstration data that goes beyond academic research scenarios into real-world manipulation and task execution. Claru's egocentric video and manipulation datasets are directly applicable, especially for the dexterous manipulation use cases RB-Y1 targets.

## Pitch Angle
Samsung backed Rainbow Robotics to commercialize humanoids — the research data from MIT and Berkeley won't scale to production training. Claru can supply commercial-scale human demonstration data: manipulation tasks, dexterous handling, bimanual activities across diverse environments. The K-Humanoid Alliance's government backing also means Rainbow Robotics has budget for data infrastructure investments. Contact through Samsung's robotics investment team or Rainbow's research-to-product team.

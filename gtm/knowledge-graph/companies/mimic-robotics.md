## Overview
Mimic Robotics (stylized "mimic") is a Zurich ETH spin-off building AI-powered dexterous robotic hands for industrial automation. Their key insight: bet on hands, not humanoids. Skilled operators wear proprietary data collection devices on factory floors to capture human manipulation demonstrations — which then train AI models via imitation learning. Raised $20M+ (oversubscribed $16M seed, Nov 2025). Founded 2024. CEO Stefan Weirich.

## Data Gap / Relationship
Mimic's business model IS human demonstration data collection — they have their own collection devices, but they're limited to their own factory floor deployments. Claru's approach (diverse human collectors across 100+ cities) is exactly what mimic needs to scale beyond a handful of partner factories. This is a very high-alignment relationship — either direct customer or strategic partnership for expanded data collection.

## Pitch Angle
Mimic's imitation learning pipeline relies on capturing expert human demonstrations — the same thing Claru does at scale. Their wearable devices work in partner factories; Claru's collectors work across 100+ cities. As mimic expands to new industrial clients, Claru can commission factory floor demonstration video in any city before mimic has a presence there. Stefan Weirich and Elvis Nava are the right contacts — pitch as "data collection at the scale your learning pipeline needs."

## Overview

GITAI is a Japanese space robotics company building autonomous robotic systems for ISS support, satellite servicing, and in-orbit construction. Based in Japan (office also in US), they completed an ISS external demonstration with their S2 dual robotic arm in May 2024 and a 16U satellite space demo in January 2025. Raised ~$83M total (latest: $15.5M in Nov 2024, led by Maezawa Fund/Yusaku Maezawa). Listed as a Priority 1 account in Claru's pipeline ("$50M+" context in the original checklist note).

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **S2 dual robotic arm**: Successfully demonstrated outside ISS (May 2024). 1.5m long, dual-arm, autonomous.
- **16U satellite**: Successful space demonstration (Jan 2025).
- **500kg-class spacecraft** (planned): Robotic arm equipped, proximity operations + satellite docking/life extension demo.
- **IVR/EVR services**: Intervehicular and Extravehicular robot services for ISS and LEO commercial stations.

## Data Gap / Relationship

GITAI is a more niche fit than terrestrial robotics. Their training data needs: space environment simulation data, robotic manipulation in microgravity conditions, teleoperation training data. This is far removed from Claru's core egocentric terrestrial collection. However, if GITAI has ground-based robotic manipulation testing (which most space robotics companies do in Earth-analog environments), Claru's robotic manipulation data could apply. Lower priority than terrestrial robotics accounts.

## Pitch Angle

This is a stretch fit. If following up: ask what data they need for their ground-based validation and testing. Space robotics typically tests on Earth first. Claru could provide robotic manipulation training data for Earth-analog scenarios. Keep expectations low; this is unlikely to be a large account.

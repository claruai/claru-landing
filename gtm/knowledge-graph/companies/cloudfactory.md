## Overview

CloudFactory is a managed workforce AI data company founded in 2010 by Mark Sears in Kathmandu, Nepal, now headquartered in Durham, North Carolina. They provide data annotation, curation, and AI consulting services across image, video, text, audio, and LiDAR modalities. Their workforce model is distinctive: 7,000+ trained, salaried data analysts (not gig/crowd workers) based primarily in Nepal and Kenya, with additional offices in the UK. They serve 170+ organizations including 11 of the world's top autonomous vehicle companies. Total funding is $78.1M (led by FTV Capital and Weatherford Capital). Corporate headcount is ~3,600-3,800 (Tracxn/PitchBook Feb 2026). Revenue estimates across data providers are wildly inconsistent ($250M-$1.1B range) and should not be trusted. In FY2026 they pivoted messaging to "AI Consulting & Platform for Scalable, Trusted AI" with a full-stack AI Platform (Data Engine, AI Engine, Training Engine) and explicit Embodied AI vertical targeting robotics and autonomous vehicles. Kevin Johnston became CEO in January 2024; founder Mark Sears transitioned to Executive Chairman.

## What They've Actually Shipped

1. **Luminar 3D LiDAR annotation (case study):** Completed complex 3D image annotation for Luminar's Hydra sensor -- labeling humans, animals, roads, unknown objects. This is their flagship AV case study. Work is remote desk annotation of pre-collected LiDAR data, not field capture.
2. **AMP Robotics annotation QC (case study):** Multi-year partnership annotating images for recycling-sorting robots. Expanded from 20 to 50 labels, 95%+ accuracy. Again: annotation of existing images, not data collection.
3. **Compound Eye camera-based AV annotation (case study):** Image annotation helping release camera-based AV technology. Remote annotation.
4. **Allvision spatial AI annotation (case study):** Smart city spatial AI data labeling. 8x faster GTM claimed.
5. **AI Platform (Data Engine + AI Engine + Training Engine):** FY2026 product pivot. Full-stack platform for data collection/curation, model fine-tuning with RLHF, and AI consulting from discovery to production deployment.
6. **Embodied AI page (cloudfactory.com/embodied-ai):** Marketing page promising to "transform messy sensor data into structured training datasets" and deliver "human-in-the-loop oversight for safety and reliability." Claims to cover perception, manipulation, and navigation data for robotics.
7. **Autonomous Vehicle vertical page (cloudfactory.com/autonomous-vehicles):** Dedicated page citing "11 of the world's top AV companies." Focus is LiDAR/image annotation, not sensor data collection.

## Data Gap (why they'd need Claru)

1. **Managed workforce model is office-bound.** Their 7,000+ analysts sit in offices in Kathmandu and Nairobi annotating data on screens. They cannot be deployed to kitchens, factories, warehouses, construction sites, or retail environments in North America or Europe to physically capture video.
2. **All case studies are annotation of pre-collected data.** Luminar, AMP Robotics, Compound Eye, Allvision -- every published case study involves labeling data that someone else already captured. CloudFactory never claims to have collected the raw sensor data itself.
3. **No field capture infrastructure.** No job postings for collectors, field operators, or sensor technicians. No mention of camera rigs, multi-sensor hardware, or capture protocols anywhere on their site or job boards.
4. **Embodied AI page is marketing aspiration, not proven delivery.** The /embodied-ai page promises to handle "messy sensor data" for robotics but provides zero case studies, zero named robotics clients (only AV annotation), and zero evidence of actual robotics data collection or delivery.
5. **VP Products who owned the embodied AI pivot has left.** Damian Rochman (VP Products) departed for Blue River Technology (John Deere's autonomous farming subsidiary). His departure may leave a gap in their robotics product strategy.
6. **New CEO is an IT services exec, not a robotics/AI specialist.** Kevin Johnston's background is enterprise IT outsourcing (DXC, HP, EDS, Capgemini, AgileThought). He brings operational scale expertise but not robotics domain knowledge.

## Pitch Angle

"Your Nepal and Kenya teams handle annotation at scale -- 7,000 analysts, 170+ clients, proven on Luminar and AMP Robotics. When an enterprise client needs physical video capture in NA/EU environments -- homes, factories, warehouses, construction sites -- that requires field-deployed collectors with hardware, protocols, and local presence. We handle that piece. 2,000+ trained collectors, 100+ cities, multi-sensor rigs, protocol-grade QA. You keep 100% of the annotation revenue. We supply the raw capture you can't produce from Kathmandu."

**Why this works:** CloudFactory is actively marketing Embodied AI as a strategic vertical and just lost their VP Products to a John Deere robotics subsidiary. Their enterprise AV clients (Luminar, Compound Eye) almost certainly need real-world video/sensor data collected in the field before CloudFactory's team can annotate it. Right now, CloudFactory either tells those clients to source their own raw data or subcontracts collection to someone else. Claru fills that gap without competing on annotation.

**Why ICP score is 82 not higher:** CloudFactory is a very large company ($78M raised, 3,800 corporate employees, 7,000+ workforce). Getting a partnership deal through a company this size takes long sales cycles and executive sponsorship. The VP Products departure creates uncertainty about who owns the embodied AI strategy. The pitch is strong on paper but execution requires reaching the right person at the right time.

**Entry point:** Kevin Johnston (CEO) directly, framed as strategic partnership. Alternatively, look for whoever replaced Damian Rochman as VP/Head of Product -- that person inherits the embodied AI roadmap gap and would be most receptive. Frame as subcontract/partnership, never competition.

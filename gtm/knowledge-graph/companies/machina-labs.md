## Overview

Machina Labs (Los Angeles, founded 2019) reinvents metal manufacturing using AI-driven robotic sheet metal forming — their RoboCraftsman platform + proprietary RoboForming technology enables rapid, software-defined production of aerospace and defense metal structures directly from digital designs. Raised $124M Series C in Feb 2026 (Woven Capital/Toyota, Lockheed Martin Ventures, Balerion Space, UAE SDF). CEO Edward Mehr is the active outreach contact in Claru's pipeline.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **RoboCraftsman**: AI + robotics platform for sheet metal forming. Combines ML, robotics, and proprietary RoboForming to produce aerospace/defense-grade metal components from digital files.
- **Large-scale intelligent factory (Feb 2026)**: First deployed at scale. 130-acre manufacturing site mass excavation deployment completed in Nov 2025.
- **Elastic Factories**: Software-defined manufacturing concept — factories that reconfigure digitally for different products.
- **Defense/aerospace customer pipeline**: US DoD, Lockheed Martin as strategic partner-investor — validated in critical industries.

## Data Gap / Relationship

Machina Labs' AI models for metal forming need: (1) video of robotic arms performing forming operations for imitation learning, (2) force-torque sensor data + video pairs for contact-rich manipulation training, (3) human operator video of sheet metal working and inspection to supplement robot data. However, Claru's primary value here is slightly different — the manufacturing context matters more than egocentric collection. The angle: Claru could source video from metal fabrication and manufacturing environments (human workers performing CNC-adjacent tasks, quality inspection, material handling) that Machina uses to improve their AI's understanding of manufacturing contexts. This is a moderate-fit account — follow up on whatever Edward responded to originally.

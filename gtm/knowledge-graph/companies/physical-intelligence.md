## Overview

Physical Intelligence (π) is a San Francisco-based robotics AI lab building foundation models and learning algorithms for physical robots. Their π0 model is a vision-language-action (VLA) model trained on 10,000+ hours of real robot data across 7 embodiments and 68 tasks. They are explicitly data-hungry — the company has publicly stated that access to large-scale multitask, multirobot data is one of their biggest constraints to training better models. They sent Claru a formal Vendor RFI in April 2026 via Sourish Jasti (Data Procurement), making them the hottest active lead in the pipeline.

## Hiring Signals

Active hiring for robotics engineers, ML researchers, and robot operators. Data collection roles not publicly visible but implied given their training scale requirements.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **π0 model**: VLA trained on 10,000+ hours of robot demonstration data across 7 embodiments and 68 manipulation tasks. State-of-the-art generalist manipulation model as of late 2024.
- **Physical AI research**: Published research on generalist robot policies and cross-embodiment transfer learning.
- Reported to be collecting their own data but the scale they need vastly exceeds internal capacity — hence the Vendor RFI to Claru.

## Data Gap / Relationship

PI is directly constrained by data volume. Their π0 model was trained on 10K hours; scaling to competitive levels with larger foundation models will require orders of magnitude more data. They cannot collect all of it internally. Sourish Jasti sent Claru a formal Vendor RFI asking specifically about: data type specs, scale (hrs/day, 3–6 month projection), diversity (environments/tasks/workers/countries), and pricing (non-exclusive, exclusive, bespoke $/hr). This is a real procurement process, not a warm lead.

**STRICT CONFIDENTIALITY**: Cannot reference PI by name or as "a major robotics lab" externally per their RFI terms.

## Pitch Angle

This is not a pitch situation — they came to us. Focus the Vendor RFI response on: (1) egocentric collection capacity and daily throughput, (2) environment diversity across NA/EU cities, (3) worker diversity and onboarding speed, (4) pricing tiers for exclusive vs. non-exclusive, (5) sample links demonstrating quality. Answer every question in the RFI precisely. Do not oversell. PI is sophisticated — they will see through vague claims.

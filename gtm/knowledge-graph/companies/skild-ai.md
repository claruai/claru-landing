## Overview

Skild AI is a Pittsburgh-based robotics foundation model company building the "Skild Brain" — an omni-bodied AI that can control any robot form factor (quadrupeds, humanoids, tabletop arms, mobile manipulators) without prior knowledge of the body form. Raised $1.83B total ($1.4B Series C in Jan 2026, led by SoftBank + NVIDIA/NVentures) at a $14B valuation. Grew from $0 to ~$30M revenue in months. Their explicit data strategy: **pre-training on human videos from the internet** + physics-based simulation. This makes them a prime Claru customer for curated, licensed human video.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Skild Brain**: Unified foundation model that controls any robot without knowing the body form. Pre-trained on human internet videos + simulation. First real omni-bodied robotics foundation model.
- **Commercial deployments**: Security/inspection, warehousing, manufacturing, delivery, construction. $30M revenue in months = real paying customers.
- **Data strategy**: Explicitly stated — "pre-training the Skild Brain on human videos on the internet and practicing in physics-based simulations." This is the clearest statement of Claru's value prop among all pipeline companies.

## Data Gap / Relationship

Skild Brain pre-trains on **human videos from the internet**. The problem with internet video: it's noisy, unlicensed, unreliable, and lacks the specific activity distribution needed for physical robot training. Claru's curated, licensed, annotated human action video is the alternative: purpose-collected, rights-clean, diverse, enriched with action labels and metadata. Skild's explicit strategy is the exact Claru use case. At $14B valuation and $30M revenue growing exponentially, this is potentially a large-scale data contract.

## Pitch Angle

Lead with their own stated strategy: "Skild Brain pre-trains on human video — we provide the licensed, curated alternative to internet scraping. 400K+ egocentric clips from 100+ cities, diverse activity types, action-labeled, rights-clean. The distribution gap between 'internet video' and 'what robots need to handle' is exactly what our collection addresses." This is a sophisticated team — use precise language about distribution coverage, annotation methodology, and licensing. Offer a sample pack focused on manipulation and locomotion tasks across diverse environments.

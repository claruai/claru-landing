## Overview

K-Scale Labs is a Y Combinator-backed open-source humanoid robotics company founded by Benjamin Bolte (ex-Meta AI, Tesla, Google, Amazon). Their mission: accelerate the path to billions of general-purpose robots by making humanoids open-source and affordable. Products range from Z-Bot (~$999, 46cm educational robot) to K-Bot (~$9K, 1.4m research humanoid). Their software stack is built in Rust with K-Scale OS running multimodal AI for vision, language, and action. Benjamin Bolte opened Claru's Wave 4 email.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Z-Bot**: Sub-$1000 humanoid robot (46cm) for students and developers. K-Scale OS (Rust-based) + multimodal AI (vision, language, action).
- **K-Bot**: $9K research humanoid (1.4m), expected Nov 2025 launch with locomotion, balance, voice commands, app-based control.
- **Stompy**: Open-source 4-ft humanoid with UMI gripper, all parts fit on 256x256 3D printer bed, BOM under $10K.
- **kscalelabs/kbot**: Open-source GitHub repo for their robot platform.
- **K-Scale OS**: Open-source OS for humanoid robots, built in Rust.

## Data Gap / Relationship

K-Scale needs training data for K-Scale OS's multimodal AI — specifically manipulation and locomotion video from diverse environments. Being open-source means they may be looking for open or affordable datasets rather than enterprise contracts. The opportunity: (1) sell affordable data packages targeted at research/developer accounts, or (2) use K-Scale as a reference customer to demonstrate Claru's accessibility to smaller robotics labs. Benjamin opened the email — the approach should acknowledge their open-source ethos and offer something accessible (sample data, not a $200K enterprise contract).

Note: Sources suggest K-Scale may have faced operational challenges in late 2025. This needs verification before committing serious resources to this account.

## Pitch Angle

Acknowledge K-Scale's open-source mission — it's their whole identity. Offer a research/startup pricing tier or a sample data contribution to the open robotics ecosystem. Frame Claru as aligned with K-Scale's goal of making robotics accessible: "We'd like to contribute egocentric manipulation data to your training pipeline — start with a free sample set, with commercial access available as you scale." This is a relationship-building play, not a big deal.

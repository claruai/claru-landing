## Overview
Veesion is a Paris-based AI company building gesture-recognition video surveillance software for retail loss prevention. Their system uses deep learning to identify suspicious shoplifting behaviors in real time across camera feeds and alerts store staff. As of mid-2025, deployed in 5,000+ stores across 25 countries. Raised €53M total (including €38M Series B in May 2025) and actively expanding into the US.

## Data Gap / Relationship
Veesion's gesture recognition models need massive labeled video datasets of human behaviors — both shoplifting actions and normal non-suspicious behavior for negative training examples. They need diverse capture across store environments, lighting conditions, demographic variation, and camera angles. As they expand into new sectors (healthcare, hospitality) they'll need new domain-specific behavior video. Claru's human activity video collection capability is a strong fit.

## Pitch Angle
Veesion just closed €38M to scale globally — their model quality bottleneck is diverse, labeled gesture video. Claru can deploy collectors in retail environments across 100+ cities to capture the specific human behaviors (concealment gestures, body language patterns, normal shopping activity) that Veesion needs at the density and diversity required to expand into new markets. Thibault David (CEO) or Benoît Koenig (US expansion) are the right contacts as they build US operations.

## Overview

Trener Robotics (formerly T-Robotics, rebranded 2026) builds Acteris — a physical AI platform that enables natural language programming of industrial robots, with pre-trained skill models for specific manufacturing tasks. Founded in 2024 by Asad Tirmizi (CEO, ex-Vicarious/Google, ex-ByteDance robotics) and Lars Tingelstad (CTO). Raised $38M+ total ($32M Series A in Feb 2026, co-led by Engine Ventures + IAG Capital Partners, with Nikon/Cadence). Supports ABB, Universal Robots, and FANUC robots. Asad Tirmizi is the active outreach contact in Claru's pipeline.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Acteris**: Physical AI platform — operators give natural language instructions → Acteris translates to robot actions. Eliminates code-based programming. Robot-agnostic.
- **Pre-trained skill models**: Task-specific expert models for industrial manipulation tasks (specific skills like pick-and-place, assembly operations, welding adjacents).
- **Robot integrations**: ABB, Universal Robots, FANUC — covers the major industrial robot brands.
- **Partnerships**: 15+ solutions/integration partners across US and Europe.
- **Nikon strategic investment**: Nikon's NFocus fund invested through Trener's round — signals manufacturing optics/precision alignment integration potential.

## Data Gap / Relationship

Trener's Acteris platform requires pre-trained skill models for specific industrial tasks. Training these skill models requires: (1) video demonstrations of humans performing the specific manufacturing tasks, (2) egocentric footage of hands manipulating industrial components, (3) diverse factory environment footage for generalization. Claru's manufacturing-adjacent egocentric collection and hand-object interaction datasets are the training data Acteris skill models are built on. Asad has been contacted — follow up with a manufacturing-specific data pitch.

## Overview

Mind Robotics is a Rivian spinout co-founded by RJ Scaringe (Rivian CEO/founder) in November 2025, focused on building industrial AI-powered robots for factory automation requiring human-like dexterity. They raised $615M total ($500M Series A co-led by Accel and a16z, March 2026) at a $2B valuation. Their thesis: train industrial robots on Rivian factory data to handle the large portion of manufacturing that classical robotics cannot (tasks requiring physical reasoning, adaptation, and dexterity). RJ Scaringe opened Claru's Wave 4 email. Highest-priority unopened account in the pipeline.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Industrial AI robot platform**: Robots deployed at Rivian factories performing dexterous tasks that classical industrial robots cannot handle (physical reasoning, adaptable grasping, task variation).
- **Training data foundation**: Rivian's manufacturing floor = massive source of real industrial video data for training. Mind Robotics has a unique built-in data advantage from Rivian factory operations.
- **Deployment target**: "Large number of robots deployed by end of 2026" per RJ Scaringe.

## Data Gap / Relationship

Mind Robotics has Rivian factory data as a primary training signal — but that data covers a very specific set of tasks. To generalize to other factories, they need: (1) diverse manufacturing/assembly task video from many environments (not just Rivian's specific factory), (2) egocentric video of humans performing dexterous assembly tasks, (3) diverse object interaction data (grasping different materials, sizes, weights). Claru's industrial-adjacent egocentric collection and object manipulation datasets are relevant. Also: Rivian's factory data is proprietary — third-party data providers like Claru are how they diversify beyond their internal moat.

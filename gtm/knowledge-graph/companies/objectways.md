## Overview

Objectways is a Scottsdale, AZ-based AI data services company founded in 2018 that has rapidly pivoted to become one of the most visible players in physical AI / egocentric data. With 2,200+ employees (targeting 3,000+), six offices across US and India, and aggressive content marketing around robotics and embodied AI, they are the closest direct competitor to Claru in the supply-side landscape. CEO Ravi Rajalingam has been featured in CNN coverage and spoke at SuperAI, positioning Objectways as the go-to egocentric data provider. They claim end-to-end collection capability, but evidence suggests their collection operation may still be scaling -- only ~50% of submitted footage meets quality thresholds.

## Hiring Signals

Objectways is in aggressive growth mode -- targeting 800+ new hires to reach 3,000+ employees. Their pivot to robotics/egocentric data is recent (CEO says he shifted focus to robotics "last year"). The company is actively hiring contractors for data collection, with the CEO publicly acknowledging that only about 50% of submitted footage meets quality standards -- suggesting they are still maturing their collection QA pipeline. The 3x US data premium indicates strong demand-side pull from American robotics companies.

## Data Gap (why they'd need Claru -- or why they're a threat)

**This company is more competitor than partner.** Objectways is trying to build exactly what Claru has: an end-to-end physical data collection + annotation pipeline. Key vulnerabilities:

1. **Quality is their Achilles heel.** CEO publicly admitted only ~50% of footage is usable. Claru's enrichment pipeline (multi-model visual QA, depth maps, structured metadata) produces higher-quality output.
2. **Annotation-first DNA.** Objectways built 2,200 employees as desk annotators first, then pivoted to collection. Their collection operations are newer and less mature than Claru's purpose-built collector network.
3. **India-heavy workforce.** Six offices across US and India, but 90% of their clients want US household data. Their India workforce handles annotation, not collection -- creating a geographic mismatch for their highest-value data type.
4. **No evidence of enrichment stack.** No mention of depth estimation, 3D reconstruction, multi-model captioning, or structured metadata pipelines. They capture and annotate, but don't enrich.
5. **Scale vs. curation.** They're optimizing for headcount (3,000+ employees) rather than data quality per clip. Claru's smaller, curated approach with enrichment may win on quality.

## Overview

iMerit is a managed-workforce AI data annotation company founded in 2012 by Radha Basu (ex-HP, 20 years). Headquartered in Los Gatos, CA, with 12 global delivery centers primarily across India (Kolkata, Ranchi, Bhubaneswar, Shillong, Vishakhapatnam, Hubballi, Coimbatore, Bangalore, Metiabruz, Baruipur) plus Thimphu, Bhutan. Employee count is ~7,200 as of Feb 2026. They serve "three of the top seven generative AI developers," eight major AV startups, U.S. federal agencies, and Fortune 100 companies. Automotive AI is their largest vertical at 60% of revenue. Raised $24.3M total from impact investors (BII, Khosla Impact, Dell Foundation, Omidyar Network). Platform is Ango Hub (SOC 2, HIPAA, ISO 27001/9001 certified). They launched an iMerit Scholars program -- a curated network of 4,000+ remote domain experts (grad-level+, 91% retention, 50% women) for GenAI fine-tuning and RLHF, with plans to scale to 10,000.

Critically for Claru: iMerit has a dedicated egocentric video data collection services page and exhibited at CVPR 2025. They market "full-time employees and Scholars capture egocentric video using standardized protocols" -- but all their delivery centers are in India and Bhutan. They cannot do NA/EU field collection. When their robotics or AV clients need real-world egocentric footage from North American kitchens, European factories, or US construction sites, iMerit has no mechanism to produce it.

## What They've Actually Shipped

1. **Ango Hub Platform:** Proprietary annotation platform with workflow automation, auto-labeling (YOLOv11, 80+ object types), multi-labeler consensus, SDK/API integration, webhook egress. Powers both in-house annotation teams and Scholars program.
2. **iMerit Scholars (launched 2025):** Curated network of 4,000+ remote domain experts for GenAI fine-tuning, RLHF, evaluation. Ango Deep Reasoning Lab (DRL) enables live collaboration between AI models and subject experts. 91% retention rate.
3. **Egocentric Video Data Collection Services (dedicated page):** Markets "full-time employees and Scholars capture egocentric video using standardized protocols." Positioned for robotic foundation model training. But delivery is from India-based centers.
4. **Coimbatore Automotive AI Center of Excellence (Dec 2024):** 12th global location. 220+ multisensor fusion experts for AV annotation (LiDAR, radar, camera fusion). "Fastest ramp-up in a new location." 60% of revenue comes from automotive AI.
5. **Video Annotation Tool automation (2024):** High-speed video labeling with automation features. Claims to "expedite AI/ML development."
6. **Medical AI vertical:** HIPAA-compliant annotation for surgical video (robotic procedures). Case study with medical device manufacturer -- 99%+ accuracy, 12% improvement in model recognition. Doctors-in-the-loop workflow.
7. **Foundation model evaluation:** "Leading foundation model company" used iMerit Scholars and Ango DRL to create original math problems to stump development models. This is RLHF/red-teaming work.
8. **AV case studies:** HD mapping acceleration through process automation. 3D perception annotation for self-driving (97% accuracy, 61% time-per-task improvement). Multiple unnamed AV clients.

## Data Gap (why they'd need Claru)

1. **All delivery centers are in India and Bhutan.** Despite marketing egocentric video collection for robotics, every iMerit delivery center is in South Asia. They cannot capture footage from North American homes, European warehouses, US construction sites, or any real-world environment where frontier robotics models will actually be deployed.
2. **"Collection" means Indian environments only.** When iMerit says "full-time employees capture egocentric video," those employees are in Kolkata, Ranchi, etc. The footage is of Indian kitchens, Indian streets, Indian daily activities. This is a domain mismatch for clients building robots for US/EU markets.
3. **No field operations infrastructure in target markets.** No collector recruitment pipeline in NA/EU, no hardware provisioning system for distributed field ops, no multi-city logistics. Their entire operational model assumes centralized delivery centers.
4. **Annotation-heavy, collection-light business model.** iMerit's core revenue (especially 60% from AV) comes from annotating data that clients bring them. The egocentric collection page is a newer offering, not yet a proven at-scale operation.
5. **Scholars are cognitive experts, not field collectors.** The 4,000+ Scholars do RLHF, math, code review, medical annotation -- desk-based knowledge work. They are not equipped for physical video capture in the field.
6. **CVPR 2025 presence signals ambition.** iMerit presented at CVPR 2025 with their "dual offering of software and services." The egocentric video page and CVPR attendance show they want to be in the physical AI data market -- but their supply-side infrastructure cannot deliver NA/EU collection.
7. **Competitive pressure from Scale AI.** Scale AI ($870M revenue 2024, tracking $2B in 2025, $14.3B Meta investment) is eating into iMerit's enterprise annotation share. Offering a collection partnership extends iMerit's value proposition without capex.

## Pitch Angle

"You're already marketing egocentric video collection for robotics, and your Ango Hub platform is world-class for the annotation side. But when your AV and robotics clients need real-world footage from North American and European environments -- not Indian delivery centers -- that's a different operational challenge. We run 2,000+ trained collectors across 100+ cities in NA/EU, with multi-sensor rigs and protocol-grade QA. You keep the annotation revenue on Ango Hub. We supply the raw capture from the environments where these robots will actually operate."

**Why this works:** iMerit is actively marketing egocentric video collection for robotics but their entire workforce is in India/Bhutan. When a frontier AI lab or AV company needs footage from US kitchens, EU factories, or NA construction sites, iMerit's delivery centers are in the wrong geography. Claru fills that exact operational gap.

**Why Jai Natarajan is the right contact:** He leads strategic BD and partnerships, has been with iMerit since 2016 (pre-growth phase), has deep Computer Vision background (Lucasfilm, Sony), and understands the visual data pipeline from capture to annotation. His BD role means he evaluates partnership opportunities that extend iMerit's capabilities without requiring them to build new infrastructure.

**Entry approach:** Lead with the egocentric video collection page. "You have the dedicated page, you presented at CVPR, your clients are asking for this. When the RFP specifies NA/EU environments, we're the collection layer that makes your proposal complete." Frame as partnership/subcontract -- Claru collects, iMerit annotates on Ango Hub. Never position as competition on annotation.

**Differentiator vs Sama pitch:** Unlike Sama (which doesn't even market collection), iMerit already claims to do it. The pitch here is geographic complementarity -- not "you can't do this" but "you can do this better with NA/EU ground truth from our network."

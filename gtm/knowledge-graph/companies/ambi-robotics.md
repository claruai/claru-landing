Their robots use computer vision and AI to sort parcels and packages at high speed for shipping/fulfillment. CEO Jim Liefer. ## Data Gap / Relationship
Pick-and-place robots need training data of objects being handled, grasped, and sorted — including rare/difficult items, unusual orientations, and deformable goods. Claru can annotate existing video or commission new capture of warehouse picking scenarios. Secondary angle: their CV model needs continuous retraining as new product categories are added — ongoing annotation work.

## Pitch Angle
Pick-and-place accuracy degrades on novel objects outside the training distribution. Claru can supply annotated manipulation video — both synthetic-to-real transfer datasets and real footage of warehouse picking scenarios across diverse product types. Jim Liefer or their ML team are the right contacts.

## Overview

Bedrock Robotics is an autonomous construction equipment company founded by ex-Waymo veterans (CEO Boris Sofman, ex-head of Waymo's self-driving trucks program; CTO Kevin Peterson; VP Eng Ajay Gummalla) + Tom Eliaz (Segment/Twilio). They build a retrofit autonomous kit that transforms existing construction equipment (excavators, etc.) into fully autonomous machines — same-day, reversible install. Emerged from stealth July 2025 with $80M; raised $270M Series B (Feb 2026) for $350M+ total. First fully operator-less excavator deployments targeted for 2026.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Autonomous retrofit kit**: Hardware + software that transforms existing excavators into fully autonomous machines. Reversible, same-day installation.
- **Supervised autonomy deployment**: Completed 130-acre manufacturing site mass excavation with supervision (Nov 2025). Moving to operator-less in 2026.
- **Waymo-pedigree autonomy stack**: Team built Waymo's self-driving truck program — deep AV technology applied to construction equipment.
- **$13T market target**: Global construction industry — enormous TAM for autonomous equipment.

## Data Gap / Relationship

Bedrock's autonomy AI needs: (1) outdoor construction site video — excavation, grading, material movement, site layout, human workers nearby, (2) operator video of skilled excavator/construction equipment use for imitation learning (what does expert excavation look like from the cab?), (3) diverse outdoor terrain and weather conditions. Claru's outdoor collection in 100+ cities could capture construction site environments, equipment operation scenarios, and site worker behavior. However, construction site access requires specialized collection protocols — this might be a bespoke collection project rather than catalog data. The Waymo pedigree means they understand large-scale data operations — they know what quality data costs.

## Pitch Angle

Construction site video is a niche Claru could own. Pitch: "We run collection operations in 100+ cities — we can mobilize collectors at active construction sites to capture autonomous equipment training scenarios: site worker interactions, terrain variation, equipment operation patterns. Purpose-built for your training pipeline, with proper site permissions and safety protocols." Position as a specialized collection partner for outdoor autonomous systems, not just a generic egocentric video supplier.

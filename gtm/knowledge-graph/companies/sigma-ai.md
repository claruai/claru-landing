## Overview

Sigma AI is a Madrid-headquartered training data company founded in 2008, positioning itself as "the human context company for generative AI." With 25,000+ annotators across 5 continents covering 600+ languages, they are one of the largest annotation workforces globally. They serve 4 of 5 global tech giants and fast-growing LLM unicorns, but their heritage is overwhelmingly language/text/audio -- physical AI and robotics annotation is a stated capability, not a proven specialty.

## Hiring Signals

Sigma is actively hiring with 1,000+ open positions listed on ZipRecruiter and their own careers portal (careers.sigma.ai). Positions skew heavily toward linguistic annotators, transcriptionists, and multi-speaker audio labeling -- consistent with their NLP/language core. No specific robotics-focused or egocentric video collection roles were found in current listings. Average hiring process takes 13 days with a linguistic test as the primary screen.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- Sub-pixel image annotation for an unnamed robotics client, improving object detection precision
- Semantic segmentation for autonomous vehicle lane detection
- 3D bounding box (cuboid) annotation for spatial understanding
- Multi-speaker audio transcription and annotation at scale
- 600+ language coverage for LLM training data

The robotics and 3D work appears to be project-based rather than a dedicated practice area. Their core volume is language data -- text, audio, translation, and NLP annotation.

## Data Gap (why they'd need Claru)

1. **No physical collection network.** Sigma's 25K workforce are desk-based annotators, not field collectors with cameras. They have zero evidence of egocentric video capture, multi-sensor rigs, or real-world data collection operations.
2. **Robotics is a side note, not a vertical.** One robotics case study snippet vs. extensive language/NLP content. Their website has no dedicated robotics or physical AI page.
3. **3D annotation is listed but not deep.** Cuboid annotation is basic -- no evidence of depth maps, point clouds, or multi-view stereo annotation pipelines that frontier robotics labs need.
4. **Frontier lab demand is shifting.** Their big tech clients are increasingly asking for physical AI data (per GTC 2026 trends), and Sigma has no supply chain for it.
5. **Geographic mismatch.** Madrid/London/Miami offices serve European and LatAm language markets well, but physical AI data collection demand is concentrated in US households and industrial environments.

## Pitch Angle (subcontractor)

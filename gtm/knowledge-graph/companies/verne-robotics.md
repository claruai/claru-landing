## Overview
Verne Robotics is a YC S25 company (SF) building AI models that teach robot arms new skills in hours. Their Nemo3 bimanual robot learns from 30 minutes of teleop data using diffusion models. Founded 2025 by Neil Nie (Columbia/Stanford, ex-Apple Vision Pro patents) and Aditya Jha (ex-Microsoft Azure Copilot). Raised $500K. First commercial customer: a biotech unicorn packaging delicate chemical vials.

## Data Gap / Relationship
Verne's 30-minute teleop approach is clever but still requires expert demonstrators. As they scale to more customers and tasks, they'll need more diverse demonstration data across different task types and environments. Claru can supply teleop demonstration data at scale for tasks beyond what Verne's small team can demonstrate.

## Pitch Angle
Verne's diffusion model approach means data quality matters enormously — 30 minutes of expert demonstration is worth more than 5 hours of mediocre demonstrations. Claru can supply expert human task demonstrations with precise annotation for the specific manipulation tasks Verne's customers need. Neil Nie or Aditya Jha are the right contacts.

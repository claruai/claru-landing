## Overview

Chef Robotics is a San Francisco startup building AI-powered robotic arms for food assembly lines. Founded in 2019 by Rajat Bhageria, they've raised $65.6M total (incl. $43M Series A in 2025 led by Avataar Ventures). Deployed at Amy's Kitchen, Sunbasket, Chef Bombay, and Cafe Spice, having served 40M+ meals. The robots hang from racks above food lines and scoop/spread/assemble meal ingredients. Rajat Bhageria opened Claru's Wave 4 email. This is a high-fit account for egocentric kitchen data.

## Hiring Signals

Active hiring post-Series A. Likely hiring ML engineers and robotics operators. Equipment financing model means they're scaling deployments rapidly.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Robotic food assembly arms**: Hang from metal racks above food lines; scoop, spread, and assemble ingredients for prepackaged meals.
- **RaaS model**: No customer CapEx — Chef Robotics finances equipment and charges per use. The $22.5M equipment financing in the Series A funds this model.
- **Scale**: 40M+ meals served across US and Canada. 4+ major food manufacturer clients.
- **Pivot story**: Earlier pivoted away from restaurant customers to food manufacturers — a bet that's paying off (see TechCrunch April 2025 deep-dive).

## Data Gap / Relationship

Chef Robotics AI needs egocentric and close-up video of food preparation tasks — scooping, spreading, portioning, ingredient handling across diverse food types, containers, temperatures, and lighting conditions. This is exactly the kind of structured egocentric collection Claru specializes in. Kitchen egocentric video was already listed as a core dataset type in Claru's catalog. The more specific data need: videos of **hands manipulating food** (scooping, pouring, spreading) in **commercial kitchen environments** with **varied ingredients and containers**. Rajat's email open signals awareness — the follow-up should lead with kitchen data.

## Pitch Angle

Rajat opened the email — a direct follow-up with kitchen data specifics. "Chef Robotics' AI needs to generalize across food types, container sizes, and kitchen environments. We've collected egocentric kitchen footage showing hand-food-container interactions across diverse ingredients and commercial settings. Happy to share a sample specific to food assembly scenarios." Don't lead with general egocentric; lead with the food-specific use case. Consider a curated kitchen/food-prep sample pack for the Touch 2 follow-up.

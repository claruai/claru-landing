## Overview

Serve Robotics (NASDAQ: SERV) is an autonomous sidewalk delivery robot company co-founded by Ali Kashani (CEO). Spun out of Uber Eats, they operate 2,000+ robots across 20 cities and 6 metros (LA to DC corridor). Raised ~$300M total, public on Nasdaq. Partners with Uber Eats and DoorDash (covering 80%+ of U.S. food delivery market). Targeting 10,000+ robots in 2026 with $26M revenue guidance (10x growth). Ali Kashani is listed as the outreach contact in Claru's pipeline.

## What They've Actually Shipped

- **Sidewalk delivery robots**: Fully autonomous outdoor navigation in urban environments — pedestrian sidewalks, crosswalks, elevation changes, weather conditions.
- **Fleet deployment**: 2,000 active robots, 20 cities, LA to DC. Adding DoorDash to Uber Eats gives 80%+ U.S. food delivery coverage.
- **Financial milestone**: First real revenue ($882K Q4 2025), 401% YoY growth — proving commercial model works.
- **NVIDIA investment and integration**: NVIDIA-backed, using physical AI data for robot training.
- **2026 target**: 10,000+ robots, $26M revenue. $25M capex for manufacturing/deployment.

## Data Gap / Relationship

Serve Robotics navigates real sidewalks — they need: (1) diverse outdoor navigation video (sidewalks, crosswalks, slopes, weather, pedestrian interactions), (2) egocentric footage from urban environments across many cities, (3) edge case video (crowded sidewalks, construction zones, weather conditions). Claru's 100+ city collection network is a direct fit — they can source outdoor navigation footage in exactly the cities where Serve operates (LA, SF, DC corridor). As Serve scales to 10K robots, their models need to generalize across environments they haven't deployed in yet — that's where Claru's diverse city coverage becomes critical.

## Pitch Angle

Lead with the city coverage angle: "You're scaling to 10,000 robots across new cities — your navigation models need to handle environments they haven't been trained on yet. We've collected outdoor footage in 100+ cities across NA, including the pedestrian environments where your robots will deploy. Happy to show you what we have for [specific cities]." This is very concrete and actionable. Serve is a public company with quarterly revenue pressure — position data as a way to reduce deployment failures and accelerate expansion.

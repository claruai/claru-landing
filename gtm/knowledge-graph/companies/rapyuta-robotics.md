## Overview
Rapyuta Robotics is a Tokyo-based cloud robotics company (ETH Zurich spin-off, 2014) building pick-assist AMRs for warehouse operations. Their rapyuta.io is the world's first cloud robotics platform — robots offload computation to the cloud. Raised $81M total (Series C led by Goldman Sachs). CEO Gajan Mohanarajah. Customers report 2x productivity gains within 5 months.

## Data Gap / Relationship
Cloud robotics means perception and AI models run in the cloud — they need training data to improve robot perception across diverse warehouse environments. Claru can supply warehouse video annotation for object recognition, navigation obstacle detection, and picking task demonstration data.

## Pitch Angle
Rapyuta's cloud platform aggregates perception data from all deployed robots — but Japanese warehouse data is different from global warehouse data. As they expand internationally, they need training data from new geographies. Claru can commission warehouse video in target markets ahead of deployments.

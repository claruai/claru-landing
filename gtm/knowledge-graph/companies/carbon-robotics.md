## Overview
Carbon Robotics builds AI-powered agricultural robots using computer vision and laser technology to destroy weeds without herbicides. Their flagship LaserWeeder uses a "Large Plant Model" (LPM) — a computer vision model trained to distinguish crops from weeds at high speed. They raised $177M+ total and are expanding beyond weeding into a broader AI robot lineup. Named to CNBC Disruptor 50 in 2025.

## What They've Actually Shipped
- LaserWeeder G2: autonomous weeding robot towed behind a tractor
- Autonomous Tractor Kit (ATK): autonomy retrofit for existing tractors
- Large Plant Model: proprietary CV model for crop/weed identification at speed
- Deployed in hundreds of farms across the US

## Data Gap / Relationship
Carbon's Large Plant Model requires labeled video/image data of crops and weeds across diverse environments — different lighting, growth stages, field conditions, soil types, geographic regions. As they develop their next "AI robot" (TBD product, 9+ months away), they'll need training data for new agricultural tasks beyond weeding. Claru's collector network can capture agricultural video in 100+ geographies.

## Pitch Angle
Carbon built their own Large Plant Model — they understand the data problem. The bottleneck for expanding to new crops, regions, or the new AI robot is labeled image/video data from real farm environments. Claru can commission agricultural data capture across North America (different crops, geographies, seasons) with the annotation precision Carbon's models need. Paul Mikesell or their ML team are the right contacts.

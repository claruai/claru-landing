## Overview

SmartOne.ai is a data annotation and AI services company founded in 2012 in Antananarivo, Madagascar by Habib Hassim and Shahysta Hassim. Now headquartered in Montreal within the Mila (Quebec AI Institute) ecosystem, with delivery centers in Madagascar, and offices in Paris and London. CEO Eric Raza (appointed Dec 2024, formerly President/COO) leads a workforce of ~1,200 employees. SmartOne provides managed annotation services across text, image, video, and audio data, with dedicated robotics & manufacturing, autonomous vehicles, and healthcare verticals. Branded "Physical AI for the Real World." The company is a Harvard Business School case study ("SmartOne: Building an AI Data Business," Oct 2021), a Mila partner, and is listed on AWS Marketplace. Revenue ~$210M (ZoomInfo estimate -- treat with caution). No disclosed VC funding rounds; appears to be bootstrapped/family-owned. They launched synthetic data services (Blender, Unity, Quixel Megascans) in March 2025 and a tech workforce staffing offering in February 2025. Their annotation workforce is trained in temporal and 3D annotation, but their entire collection capability is desk-based annotation in Madagascar facilities -- they have zero field collection capability in NA/EU environments.

## What They've Actually Shipped

1. **Synthetic Data Services (Mar 2025):** Launched synthetic data generation using Blender, Quixel Megascans, and Unity. Targets computer vision, NLP, autonomous systems, document processing. Markets to robotics, self-driving, and medical imaging. Framed as bridging the simulation-to-reality gap. GDPR/HIPAA compliant alternative to real data.
2. **Tech Workforce Services (Feb 2025):** Staffing-as-a-service offering providing pre-validated developers, engineers, and data scientists. Bilingual (French/English). Specializing in React, Angular, Flutter, React Native, Python, Java, C#. Positions SmartOne beyond annotation into BPO/staff augmentation.
3. **Robotics & Manufacturing Vertical Page:** Dedicated page marketing annotation for defect detection, automation processes, product traceability, supply chain management, AI-powered robotics. Claims high-precision data labeling for robotics AI. But this is all annotation of existing data -- not collection.
4. **AWS Marketplace Listing:** Available for procurement through AWS Marketplace with usage-based billing. Lowers procurement friction for enterprise clients.
5. **Mila Partnership:** Official partner of Mila (Quebec AI Institute, founded by Yoshua Bengio). Leverages deep learning research collaboration. Legitimizes SmartOne in the Montreal AI ecosystem.
6. **Harvard Business School Case Study (Oct 2021):** "SmartOne: Building an AI Data Business" (Case 622-059). Authored by Pippa Armerding, Gamze Yucaoglu, Fares Khrais. Covers SmartOne's strategy for positioning in the AI value chain.
7. **Major Rebrand (2024):** Logo update and repositioning around "Physical AI for the Real World" branding. Coincided with HQ relocation to Montreal and Eric Raza's appointment.
8. **Nadeau Innovations Acquisition (Feb 2024):** Acquired Dr. Nicholas Nadeau's robotics/AI consultancy and appointed him CTO. Brought robotics credibility (1X Technologies, humanoid robots, OpenAI-backed). However, Nadeau appears to have departed by Sep 2025 to co-found Onix.

## Data Gap (why they'd need Claru)

1. **Zero physical collection capability.** SmartOne's entire workforce is in Madagascar facilities. They annotate data that clients bring to them. They do not collect video, sensor data, or any physical-world captures in target environments.
2. **Madagascar cannot do NA/EU field capture.** When a robotics client needs egocentric video from North American warehouses, European factories, or construction sites, SmartOne has no mechanism to produce that raw footage. The environments, equipment, cultural context, and legal frameworks are completely different.
3. **Annotation-only delivery model.** Their entire stack -- HITL annotation, synthetic data generation, tech workforce -- is designed for processing data that already exists or generating simulated data. There is no real-world collection pipeline, no hardware provisioning, no field protocol system.
4. **Synthetic data is not a substitute for real-world capture.** Their March 2025 synthetic data launch (Blender/Unity) generates simulated environments. But frontier robotics and physical AI models need real-world data from actual target environments -- kitchens, factories, construction sites, streets. Synthetic fills gaps; it doesn't replace purpose-collected field data.
5. **CTO departure weakens robotics credibility.** Dr. Nadeau (ex-1X CTO) gave SmartOne legitimate robotics credentials. His apparent departure to Onix in Sep 2025 leaves a gap in their physical AI positioning. They're marketing "Physical AI for the Real World" without the robotics-native technical leadership they once had.
6. **Eric Raza's expansion ambitions need a collection partner.** Raza was appointed specifically to drive NA/EU expansion. He comes from Ericsson and Bounteous -- enterprise transformation, not data collection. When enterprise robotics clients in NA/EU ask for end-to-end data (collection + annotation), SmartOne can only deliver half.
7. **"Physical AI for the Real World" branding creates an expectation they can't fulfill alone.** Their marketing positions them as a physical AI data partner, but their actual capability is desk-based annotation in Madagascar. The gap between brand promise and delivery capability is exactly where Claru fits.

## Pitch Angle

"You're branding as 'Physical AI for the Real World' and expanding into NA/EU -- that's exactly the market we serve. When your robotics clients need the raw egocentric video from real North American and European environments, that's a different operation from what your Madagascar team delivers. We handle the capture piece -- 2,000+ trained collectors, 100+ cities, multi-sensor rigs, protocol-grade QA. You keep the annotation revenue, we supply the raw capture you can't produce in-house."

**Why this works:** SmartOne is actively positioning as a physical AI data company with their rebrand and Eric Raza's NA/EU expansion mandate. But their delivery capability is annotation-only from Madagascar. When an enterprise RFP includes real-world video collection from target environments, SmartOne either turns it down, generates synthetic data as a substitute, or asks the client to self-collect. Claru fills that exact gap without competing on annotation.

**Why Eric Raza is the right contact:** He was brought in specifically to drive North American and European growth. He understands enterprise transformation from his Ericsson days ($7B program, 6,000 people). He knows that winning enterprise robotics deals in NA/EU requires capabilities beyond what a Madagascar annotation center can deliver. A collection partnership extends SmartOne's value proposition into end-to-end data delivery without capex.

**Entry approach:** Lead with the physical AI positioning alignment. "You've branded around Physical AI for the Real World -- we're the collection layer that makes that real. Your Madagascar team handles annotation at world-class quality. Our 2,000+ collectors in 100+ North American and European cities handle the field capture. Together you can respond to end-to-end RFPs that neither of us could win alone." Frame as partnership/subcontract. Never position as competition.

**Alternative angle via Nadeau (if still connected):** If Dr. Nadeau retains any advisory relationship with SmartOne, he's the ideal technical validator. He ran humanoid robot deployment at 1X -- he knows intimately that real-world physical AI requires purpose-collected data from target environments, not just annotation or synthetic generation.

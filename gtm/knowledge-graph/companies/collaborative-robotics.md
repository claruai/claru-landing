## Overview
Collaborative Robotics (Cobot) builds AI-equipped autonomous mobile robots that navigate and work alongside humans in manufacturing, supply chain, and healthcare. Founded 2022 by Brad Porter (ex-Amazon VP Robotics), they raised $140M+ in under 2 years. Their Proxie robot is an AI-powered mobile manipulator for material handling. The company emphasizes "virtuous cycle" data flywheel — more robots in field = better AI.

## Data Gap / Relationship
Brad Porter explicitly said "more robots in the field lead to improved AI" — they're building a data flywheel. But early-stage companies with limited field deployments need external data to bootstrap training before the flywheel spins up. Claru can provide diverse human task demonstration video and warehouse/manufacturing environment footage that Cobot's Proxie needs to learn real-world navigation and manipulation behaviors.

## Pitch Angle
Cobot's Proxie needs to generalize across diverse manufacturing and warehouse environments — that requires training data from environments where the robot isn't deployed yet. Claru can collect video of human workers performing the exact tasks Proxie is meant to automate (material handling, transport, picking) in production environments across 100+ cities. Position as the data accelerant for their virtuous cycle before their own fleet is large enough to generate it themselves.

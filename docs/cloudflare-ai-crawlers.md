# Cloudflare AI Crawler Management

## Problem

Cloudflare's "AI Crawler Management" feature, when set to **Block**, prepends `Disallow` rules in the served `robots.txt` for the following user-agents:

- `GPTBot` (OpenAI / ChatGPT search)
- `ClaudeBot` (Anthropic / Claude search)
- `Applebot-Extended` (Apple Intelligence)
- `PerplexityBot` (Perplexity AI)
- `Google-Extended` (Google Gemini training)

These rules are injected **at the Cloudflare edge** and override whatever `robots.txt` our Next.js app generates from `src/app/robots.ts`. Even though our code explicitly allows GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Applebot-Extended, Cloudflare's edge layer can prepend blanket `Disallow: /` rules before our app's rules are served.

## Why It Matters

These are the AI systems that power conversational search (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Apple Siri/Spotlight). When a user asks "who provides robotics training data?" or "best egocentric video dataset provider," these crawlers need to be able to read our pages to cite Claru in their answers. Blocking them means Claru is invisible in AI-generated search results -- a growing share of discovery traffic for B2B buyers.

## The Fix

1. Go to **Cloudflare Dashboard** for `claru.ai`
2. Navigate to **Security > Bots > AI Crawlers** (or **Security > Bots > Bot Management** depending on plan)
3. Under **AI Crawler Management**, set the following crawlers to **Allow**:
   - GPTBot
   - ClaudeBot
   - Applebot-Extended
   - PerplexityBot
4. Keep the following crawlers **Blocked** (training-only scrapers we do not want indexing our content):
   - Google-Extended (Gemini training scraper -- distinct from Googlebot)
   - CCBot (Common Crawl)
   - Bytespider (ByteDance)
   - meta-externalagent (Meta AI training)

## How to Verify

After making the change, wait 5-10 minutes for Cloudflare's cache to clear, then verify:

```bash
# Fetch the live robots.txt
curl -s https://claru.ai/robots.txt

# Check specifically for GPTBot and ClaudeBot rules
curl -s https://claru.ai/robots.txt | grep -A2 -E "GPTBot|ClaudeBot|Applebot-Extended|PerplexityBot"
```

### What to look for

- GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Applebot-Extended should each have `Allow: /`
- There should be **no** `Disallow: /` rule under those user-agents (other than the specific private paths)
- If you see a blanket `Disallow: /` under GPTBot or ClaudeBot, Cloudflare is still injecting rules

## Source of Truth

Our robots.txt is generated by Next.js from `src/app/robots.ts`. This file is the canonical source of truth for crawler access rules. Cloudflare should pass through our app's robots.txt without modification for the crawlers we explicitly allow.

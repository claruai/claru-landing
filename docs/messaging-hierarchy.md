**1. VFX-trained annotators for temporal consistency**
Claru recruits from visual effects and post-production — people who already think in frame sequences, motion continuity, and temporal coherence. This is a hiring pipeline no competitor has built.

**2. Embedded inside your eng workflow, not behind a portal**
Claru teams join client Slack, attend daily standups, participate in architecture reviews, and iterate on annotation guidelines in real-time. The "portal and a prayer" contrast is the sharpest articulation — use it everywhere.

**3. Full-pipeline for video and robotics — from capture to red team**
Most annotation vendors only label. Claru sources data (egocentric video, synthetic generation, licensing), labels it (frame-level annotation, RLHF), and validates it (red teaming, benchmark curation, bias detection).

## 7. VOICE GUARDRAILS

**Rule 1: Speak in frames, not features.**
Ground claims in video/robotics reality — frames, temporal sequences, manipulation data, egocentric capture. If it could appear on Surge's or Scale's site unchanged, rewrite it.

**Rule 2: Show the room, not the resume.**
Describe the working relationship — "in your Slack," "at your architecture review" — not credentials. The embedded model is felt, not credentialed.

**Rule 3: Terminal voice, not marketing voice.**
Direct, compressed, slightly technical, zero fluff. No "revolutionizing," no "cutting-edge," no "unlock the power of." Write it like a commit message, not a press release.

## What We Did
Launched a production-ready collection workflow within days. Translated nuanced research-level movement specifications into contributor-friendly task instructions. Supported capture across smartphones, GoPros, and wearable cameras.

An in-house QA team ensured every submission met framing, motion, and duration requirements. Real-time dashboards enabled dynamic rebalancing across geographies and activity types. Coverage spanned household tasks, fine-grained manipulation actions, walking, driving, and cooking.

## Why It Matters
For robotics and world models, progress depends on how quickly real-world data can be collected and put to use. This engagement demonstrated that large-scale first-person video collection can be both fast and cost-effective — without sacrificing the research-grade quality that frontier labs require.

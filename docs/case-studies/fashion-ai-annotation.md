## Summary
Partnered with a fashion AI platform to strengthen SKU conditioning and visual product recognition. Deployed 1,000+ trained annotators with a structured taxonomy across apparel, accessories, and household products. Each item received product labels plus short and long descriptions. A multi-layer QA process ensured consistency at scale, materially improving model robustness and recognition accuracy.

## Key Metrics
- 3M+ images annotated
- 1,000+ annotators deployed
- Multi-category structured taxonomy

## What We Did
Deployed a distributed workforce of 1,000+ annotators and QA reviewers, supported by clear guidelines and standardized taxonomies. Every annotation passed through a multi-layer quality review before acceptance.

Annotators labeled fashion and lifestyle imagery sourced from curated product URLs, covering both product images and lifestyle images. They applied structured product labels and authored short and long descriptions for each item.

## Why It Matters
Success in fashion AI depends on disciplined annotation workflows and rigorous quality control. When training models for SKU conditioning and visual recognition, consistency is as critical as volume — and scaling one without the other creates hidden quality debt that undermines downstream performance.

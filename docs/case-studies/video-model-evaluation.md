## Summary
Designed and executed a rigorous ELO-based human evaluation framework to identify the best-performing video generation models globally and by input modality. Compared 51 configurations across text-to-video, image-to-video, and video-to-video. Completed 39,000 pairwise evaluations. A pilot study validated which metrics produce reliable human judgments before scaling.

## Key Metrics
- 51 model configurations compared
- 39,000 human pairwise evaluations
- 3 input modalities (T2V, I2V, V2V), 120 prompts, ~6,000 generated videos

## What We Did
Phase 1 was a pilot study testing 6 candidate evaluation metrics with 5 annotators to validate which produced reliable inter-annotator agreement, measured by Krippendorff's alpha.

Phase 2 scaled to a full ELO-based evaluation with Swiss-style pairing and dynamic ranking across 6 rounds. A fixed baseline prevented drift. Separate rankings were maintained per modality to ensure actionable, granular results.

## Why It Matters
Replaced uncertain aggregate metrics with statistically defensible model leadership rankings. This enables deploying the best configuration per customer workflow while reducing regression risk — turning model selection from an art into a governed, evidence-based process.

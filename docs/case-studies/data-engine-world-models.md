## Summary
Built a custom data collection and annotation engagement for a robotics-focused AI company developing world models. The system handled first-person video capture of everyday human activities via mobile and GoPro. All videos were labeled with structured activity classifications and enriched metadata, then validated through quality review.

## Key Metrics
- Thousands of hours of first-person video
- Custom capture and ingestion system
- Structured activity labels + metadata

## What We Did
Built a dedicated capture and ingestion system from scratch. Contributors recorded first-person video from phones or GoPros and uploaded directly to a custom portal.

A clear taxonomy of target actions was defined in close collaboration with the customer. Annotators used a dedicated interface to upload, label, and apply metadata to each submission. A specialized QA team validated all submissions against the agreed taxonomy and quality standards.

## Why It Matters
Purpose-built capture systems and close collaboration on action definitions are critical for delivering high-value training data in emerging AI domains. Off-the-shelf tools cannot accommodate the specificity that frontier robotics and world model research demands — the data pipeline must be engineered alongside the research itself.

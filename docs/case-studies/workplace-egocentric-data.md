## Summary
Piloted a new approach to robotics data: capturing first-person video directly inside active workplaces. Collaborated with businesses globally across food service, tailoring, cleaning, furniture assembly, and factory settings. Workers captured egocentric footage with smartphones during normal workflow. The pilot validated that real workplaces can serve as a scalable source of robotics training data.

## Key Metrics
- Global geographic coverage
- Multiple workplace types (food, tailoring, cleaning, factories)
- Smartphone-only capture, minimal disruption

## What We Did
Embedded data capture into real-world business operations. Onboarded business owners and workers as a lightweight side-revenue opportunity, keeping disruption to normal workflows minimal.

Tasks were designed for handheld smartphone recording. Research guidance was translated into practical instructions that respected workplace realities — space constraints, safety requirements, and varying levels of technical comfort. Activity coverage and task diversity were tracked throughout.

## Why It Matters
This pilot demonstrated that real workplaces can be a scalable, cost-efficient dataset source for robotics training. The behaviors captured in active work environments — the improvisation, the physical constraints, the contextual decision-making — cannot be replicated in staged settings. For general-purpose robotics, this kind of data is essential.

## Summary
Annotated 105,000 video clips in seven days for AI-powered content selection and deduplication. The task was binary organic/not-organic classification. Early batches revealed inconsistent decisions, so the annotation framework was rapidly redesigned mid-project. Abstract definitions were replaced with criteria-driven decision paths. The final dataset was approved for direct model training with zero downstream rework.

## Key Metrics
- 105,000 video clips in 7 days
- 4 confidence tiers delivered
- Zero downstream rework needed

## What We Did
Identified quality risks in early annotation batches and rapidly re-engineered the workflow. Abstract definitions were replaced with clear Yes/No decision paths. Self-reported confidence scoring was removed in favor of automated confidence tiers. The annotator UX was simplified with embedded examples at each decision point.

Early outputs were revalidated under the new framework. Pre-production quality checkpoints were introduced to catch issues before they propagated.

## Why It Matters
Training data quality directly determines model performance and time-to-market. Resolving subjectivity early in the annotation process prevents hidden quality debt that only surfaces after deployment — when the cost of rework is orders of magnitude higher.

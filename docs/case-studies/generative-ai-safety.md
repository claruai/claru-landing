## Summary
Executed a multi-modal data labeling program for a leading generative AI company to validate, strengthen, and operationalize content safety at scale. Annotators reviewed text and video outputs that had already passed automated moderation. A 2% violation rate threshold served as the success benchmark. The program delivered defensible evidence of safety performance for audits and governance.

## Key Metrics
- 205,000 safety annotations completed
- Coverage across multiple products and modalities
- Continuous violation tracking against 2% benchmark

## What We Did
Built a high-throughput, quality-controlled annotation workflow focused exclusively on residual risk — only reviewing outputs that had already passed automated moderation. Annotators evaluated text and video outputs against safety categories including nudity/NSFW, violence, and hate/harassment.

Each output received a clear binary safety label. Live dashboards enabled ongoing monitoring and rapid identification of emerging patterns across products and modalities.

## Why It Matters
As generative AI moves toward mission-critical deployment, safety must be measured — not assumed. Human-labeled data provides the ground truth needed to validate automated moderation pipelines, satisfy audit requirements, and build the governance frameworks that regulators and enterprise customers demand.
